### COOLING SYSTEM DIAGNOSIS—DIESEL ENGINE—CONTINUED

| CONDITION | POSSIBLE CAUSES | CORRECTION |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| TEMPERATURE GAUGE READING IS INCONSISTENT (FLUCTUATES, CYCLES OR IS ERRATIC) | 1. During cold weather operation, with the heater blower in the high position, the gauge reading may drop slightly. Fluctuation is also influenced by loads, outside temperature and extended idle time with diesel engines.<br>2. Temperature gauge or engine mounted gauge sensor defective or shorted. Also, corroded or loose wiring in this circuit.<br>3. Gauge reading rises when vehicle is brought to a stop after heavy use (engine still running).<br>4. Gauge reading high after restarting a warmed-up (hot) engine.<br>5. Coolant level low in radiator (air will build up in the cooling system causing the thermostat to open late).<br>6. Cylinder head gasket leaking allowing exhaust gas to enter cooling system causing thermostat to open late.<br>7. Water pump impeller loose on shaft.<br>8. Loose accessory drive belt (water pump slipping).<br>9. Air leak on the suction side of water pump allows air to build up in cooling system causing thermostat to open late. | 1. A normal condition. No correction is necessary.<br>2. Check operation of gauge and repair if necessary. Refer to Group 8E, Instrument Panel And Gauges.<br>3. A normal condition. No correction is necessary. Gauge reading should return to normal range after vehicle is driven.<br>4. A normal condition. No correction is necessary. The gauge should return to normal range after a few minutes of engine operation.<br>5. Check and correct coolant leaks. Refer to Testing Cooling System For Leaks in this group.<br>6. (a) Check for cylinder head gasket leaks with a commercially available Block Leak Tester. Repair as necessary.<br>(b) Check for coolant in the engine oil. Inspect for white steam emitting from exhaust system. Repair as necessary.<br>7. Check water pump and replace as necessary. Refer to Water Pumps in this group.<br>8. Refer to Engine Accessory Drive Belts in this group. Check and correct as necessary.<br>9. Locate leak and repair as necessary. |
