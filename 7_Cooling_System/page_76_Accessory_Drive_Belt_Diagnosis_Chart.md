# DIAGNOSIS AND TESTING (Continued)

## ACCESSORY DRIVE BELT DIAGNOSIS CHART

| CONDITION | POSSIBLE CAUSES | CORRECTION |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| RIB CHUNKING (One or more ribs has separated from belt body) | 1. Foreign objects imbedded in pulley grooves<br>2. Installation damage | 1. Remove foreign objects from pulley grooves. Replace belt<br>2. Replace belt |
| RIB OR BELT WEAR | 1. Pulley misaligned<br>2. Abrasive environment<br>3. Rusted pulley(s)<br>4. Sharp or jagged pulley groove tips<br>5. Belt rubber deteriorated | 1. Align pulley(s)<br>2. Clean pulley(s). Replace belt if necessary<br>3. Clean rust from pulley(s)<br>4. Replace pulley. Inspect belt<br>5. Replace belt |
| BELT SLIPS | 1. Belt slipping because of insufficient tension<br>2. Belt or pulley exposed to substance that has reduced friction (belt dressing, oil, ethylene glycol)<br>3. Driven component bearing failure (seizure)<br>4. Belt glazed or hardened from heat and excessive slippage | 1. Inspect/Replace tensioner if necessary<br>2. Replace belt and clean pulleys<br>3. Replace faulty component or bearing<br>4. Replace belt |
| LONGITUDINAL BELT CRACKING | 1. Belt has mistracked from pulley groove<br>2. Pulley groove tip has worn away rubber to tensile member | 1. Replace belt<br>2. Replace belt |
| "GROOVE JUMPING" (Belt does not maintain correct position on pulley) | 1. Incorrect belt tension<br>2. Pulley(s) not within design tolerance<br>3. Foreign object(s) in grooves<br>4. Pulley misalignment<br>5. Belt cordline is broken | 1. Inspect/Replace tensioner if necessary<br>2. Replace pulley(s)<br>3. Remove foreign objects from grooves<br>4. Align component<br>5. Replace belt |
| BELT BROKEN (Note: Identify and correct problem before new belt is installed) | 1. Incorrect belt tension<br>2. Tensile member damaged during belt installation<br>3. Severe misalignment<br>4. Bracket, pulley, or bearing failure | 1. Replace Inspect/Replace tensioner if necessary<br>2. Replace belt<br>3. Align pulley(s)<br>4. Replace defective component and belt |
