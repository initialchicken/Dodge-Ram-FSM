# COOLING SYSTEM DIAGNOSIS—DIESEL ENGINE—CONTINUED

## DIAGNOSIS AND TESTING (Continued)

| CONDITION | POSSIBLE CAUSES | CORRECTION |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| TEMPERATURE GAUGE READS HIGH. COOLANT MAY OR MAY NOT BE LOST OR LEAKING FROM COOLING SYSTEM - CONT. | 6. Coolant level low in radiator but not in coolant reserve/overflow tank. This means the radiator is not drawing coolant from the coolant reserve/overflow tank as the engine cools. As the engine cools, a vacuum is formed in the cooling system of the engine and radiator. If radiator cap seals are defective, or cooling system has leaks, a vacuum can not be formed.<br>7. Freeze point of antifreeze not correct. Mixture may be too rich.<br>8. Coolant not flowing through system.<br>9. Radiator or A/C condenser fins are dirty or clogged.<br>10. Radiator core is corroded or plugged.<br>11. Aftermarket A/C installed without proper radiator.<br>12. Dragging brakes.<br>13. Bug screen is being used reducing airflow.<br>14. Thermostat partially or completely shut. This is more prevalent on high mileage vehicles.<br>15. Thermal viscous fan drive not operating properly.<br>16. Cylinder head gasket leaking.<br>17. Heater core leaking. | 6. (a) Check condition of radiator cap and cap seals. Refer to Radiator Cap in this group. Replace cap if necessary.<br>(b) Check condition of radiator filler neck. If neck is bent or damaged, replace radiator.<br>(c) Check the condition of the hose from the radiator to the coolant tank. It should fit tight at both ends without any kinks or tears. Replace hose if necessary.<br>(d) Check coolant reserve/overflow tank and tank hoses for blockage. Repair as necessary.<br>7. Check antifreeze. Refer to Coolant section of this group. Adjust antifreeze-to-water ratio as required.<br>8. Check for coolant flow at radiator filler neck with some coolant removed, engine warm and thermostat open. Coolant should be observed flowing through radiator. If flow is not observed, determine reason for lack of flow and repair as necessary.<br>9. Clean insects or debris. Refer to Radiator Cleaning in this group.<br>10. Have radiator re-cored or replaced.<br>11. Install proper radiator.<br>12. Check and correct as necessary. Refer to Group 5, Brakes in the manual text.<br>13. Remove bug screen.<br>14. Check thermostat operation and replace as necessary. Refer to Thermostats in this group.<br>15. Check fan drive operation and replace if necessary. Refer to Viscous Fan Drive in this group.<br>16. Check for cylinder head gasket leaks. Refer to Testing Cooling System For Leaks in this group. For repair, refer to Group 9, Engines.<br>17. Check heater core for leaks. Refer to Group 24, Heating and Air Conditioning. Repair as necessary. |
