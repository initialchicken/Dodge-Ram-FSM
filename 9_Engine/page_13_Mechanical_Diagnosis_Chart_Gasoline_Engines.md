## DIAGNOSIS AND TESTING (Continued)

### MECHANICAL DIAGNOSIS CHART—GASOLINE ENGINES

| CONDITION | POSSIBLE CAUSES | CORRECTION |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| NOISY VALVES/LIFTERS | 1. High or low oil level in crankcase<br>2. Thin or diluted oil<br>3. Low oil pressure<br>4. Dirt in tappets/lash adjusters<br>5. Bent push rod(s)<br>6. Worn rocker arms<br>7. Worn tappets/lash adjusters<br>8. Worn valve guides<br>9. Excessive runout of valve seats or valve faces | 1. Check for correct oil level. Adjust oil level by draining or adding as needed<br>2. Change oil (Refer to Engine Oil Service in this group)<br>3. Check engine oil level. If ok, Perform oil pressure test. Refer to this group for engine oil pressure test/specifications<br>4. Clean/replace hydraulic tappets/lash adjusters<br>5. Install new push rods<br>6. Inspect oil supply to rocker arms and replace worn arms as needed<br>7. Install new hydraulic tappets/lash adjusters<br>8. Inspect all valve guides and replace as necessary<br>9. Grind valves and seats |
| CONNECTING ROD NOISE | 1. Insufficient oil supply<br>2. Low oil pressure<br>3. Thin or diluted oil<br>4. Excessive connecting rod bearing clearance<br>5. Connecting rod journal out of round<br>6. Misaligned connecting rods | 1. Check engine oil level. (Refer to group 0, Lubrication and Maintenance)<br>2. Check engine oil level. If ok, Perform oil pressure test. Refer to this group for engine oil pressure test/specifications<br>3. Change oil to correct viscosity. Refer to this group for correct procedure/engine oil specifications<br>4. Measure bearings for correct clearance with plasti-gauge. Repair as necessary<br>5. Replace crankshaft or grind journals<br>6. Replace bent connecting rods |
| MAIN BEARING NOISE | 1. Insufficient oil supply<br>2. Low oil pressure<br>3. Thin or diluted oil<br>4. Excessive main bearing clearance<br>5. Excessive end play | 1. Check engine oil level. (Refer to group 0, Lubrication and Maintenance)<br>2. Check engine oil level. If ok, Perform oil pressure test. Refer to this group for engine oil pressure test/specifications<br>3. Change oil to correct viscosity. Refer to this group for correct procedure/engine oil specifications<br>4. Measure bearings for correct clearance. Repair as necessary<br>5. Check crankshaft thrust bearing for excessive wear on flanges |
