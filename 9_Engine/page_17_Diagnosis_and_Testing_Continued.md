# BR — ENGINE 9 - 17

## DIAGNOSIS AND TESTING (Continued)

| CONDITION | POSSIBLE CAUSES | CORRECTION |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| ROUGH IDLE (IRREGULARLY FIRING OR ENGINE SHAKING) | 1. If engine is cold, intake heater system defective.<br>2. Idle speed too low for the accessories.<br>3. Low pressure fuel problem.<br>4. Engine mounts damaged or loose.<br>5. High pressure fuel leaks.<br>6. Air in the fuel system.<br>7. Sticking needle valve in an injector. | 1. Refer to intake heater system (see Group 14, Fuel System)<br>2. Adjust the idle speed.<br>3. Check for proper lift pump pressure.<br>4. Repair or replace mounts.<br>5. Correct leaks in the high pressure lines, fittings or delivery valves.<br>6. Bleed the fuel system and eliminate the source of the air.<br>7. Check and replace the injector with the sticking needle valve. |
| ENGINE RUNS ROUGH | 1. Fuel injection line(s) leaking.<br>2. Air in the fuel or the fuel supply is inadequate.<br>3. Contaminated fuel<br>4. Incorrect valve operation.<br>5. Injection pump timing incorrect.<br>6. Improperly operating injectors.<br>7. Defective injection pump delivery valve.<br>8. Camshaft out of time.<br>9. Damaged camshaft or tappets. | 1. Correct leaks in high pressure lines, fittings, injector sealing washers or delivery valves.<br>2. Check the flow through the filter and bleed the system. Locate and eliminate the air source. Check pressures.<br>3. Verify by operating the engine with clean fuel from a temporary supply tank. Check for presence of gasoline, replace fuel/water separator filter.<br>4. Check for a bent push rod and adjust valves. Replace push rod if necessary.<br>5. Check and time pump if necessary. Refer to group 14, Fuel System.<br>6. Replace inoperative injectors.<br>7. Repair or replace injection pump.<br>8. Check/correct gear train timing alignment.<br>9. Inspect camshaft valve lift. Replace camshaft and tappets. |
| ENGINE RPM WILL NOT REACH RATED SPEED | 1. Intake air system leaks<br>2. Engine overload<br>3. Improperly operating tachometer.<br>4. Throttle linkage worn or incorrectly adjusted. | 1. Verify air duct connections are tight and secure.<br>2. Verify high idle speed without the load. Investigate operation to be sure correct gear is being used.<br>3. Verify engine speed with hand-held tachometer, correct as required.<br>4. Adjust linkage for stop to stop fuel control lever travel. Replace linkage if necessary. |
