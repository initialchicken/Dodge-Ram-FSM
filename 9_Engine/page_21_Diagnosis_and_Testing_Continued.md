# BR
## DIAGNOSIS AND TESTING (Continued)

| CONDITION | POSSIBLE CAUSES | CORRECTION |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| COMPRESSION KNOCKS | 1. Air in the fuel system.<br>2. Poor quality fuel or water/gasoline contaminated fuel.<br>3. Engine overloaded.<br>4. Incorrect injection pump timing.<br>5. Improperly operating injectors. | 1. Bleed the fuel system (refer to Group 14, Fuel System).<br>2. Verify by operating from a temporary tank with good fuel. Clean and flush the fuel supply tanks. Replace fuel/water separator filter.<br>3. Verify the engine load rating is not being exceeded.<br>4. Check and time injection pump (refer to Group 14, Fuel System).<br>5. Check and replace inoperative injectors. |
| EXCESSIVE VIBRATION | 1. Loose or broken engine mounts.<br>2. Damaged fan or improperly operating accessories.<br>3. Improperly operating vibration damper.<br>4. Improperly operating viscous fan drive.<br>5. Worn or damaged generator bearing.<br>6. Flywheel housing misaligned.<br>7. Loose or broken power component.<br>8. Worn or unbalanced driveline components. | 1. Replace engine mounts.<br>2. Check and replace the vibrating components.<br>3. Inspect/replace the vibration damper.<br>4. Inspect/replace the fan drive.<br>5. Check/replace the generator.<br>6. Check/correct flywheel alignment.<br>7. Inspect the crankshaft and rods for damage that causes an unbalance. repair/replace as required.<br>8. Check/repair driveline components. |
| EXCESSIVE ENGINE NOISES | 1. Drive belt squeal, insufficient tension or abnormally high loading.<br>2. Intake air or exhaust leaks.<br>3. Excessive valve lash.<br>4. Turbocharger noise.<br>5. Gear train noise.<br>6. Power function knock. | 1. Check the automatic tensioner and inspect the drive belt. Make sure water pump, tensioner pulley, fan hub and generator turn freely.<br>2. Refer to Excessive Exhaust smoke (Engine Diagnosis Performance).<br>3. Adjust valves. Make sure the push rods are not bent and rocker levers or adjusting screws are not severely worn. Replace bent or severely worn pads.<br>4. Check turbocharger impeller and turbine wheel for housing contact. Repair/replace as required.<br>5. Visually inspect and measure gear backlash. Replace gears as required.<br>6. Check/replace rod and main bearings. |
| GENERATOR NOT CHARGING OR INSUFFICIENT CHARGING | 1. Loose or corroded battery.<br>2. Generator belt slipping.<br>3. Generator pulley loose on shaft.<br>4. Improperly operating generator. | 1. Clean/tighten battery connection.<br>2. Check/replace automatic belt tensioner. Check/replace drive belt.<br>3. Tighten pulley.<br>4. Check/replace generator. |
