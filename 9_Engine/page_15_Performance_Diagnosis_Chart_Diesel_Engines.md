## DIAGNOSIS AND TESTING (Continued)

### SERVICE DIAGNOSIS—DIESEL ENGINES

#### PERFORMANCE DIAGNOSIS CHART—DIESEL ENGINES

| CONDITION | POSSIBLE CAUSES | CORRECTION |
|-----------|-----------------|------------|
| ENGINE WILL NOT CRANK OR CRANKS SLOWLY | 1. Batteries weak or dead.<br>2. No voltage to starter solenoid or inoperative solenoid.<br>3. Starting circuit connections loose or corroded.<br>4. Neutral safety switch or starter relay inoperative.<br>5. Starting motor operating, but not cranking the engine<br>6. Crankshaft rotation restricted. | 1. Check battery voltage. Replace battery if charge cannot be held.<br>2. Check voltage to the solenoid. If necessary, replace starter solenoid.<br>3. Clean and tighten connections<br>4. Check starter relay supply voltage and proper operation of neutral safety switch if equipped. Replace defective parts.<br>5. Remove the starter motor, check for broken flywheel teeth or a broken starter motor spring.<br>6. Attempt to rotate engine with barring tool. If engine does not turn, suspect internal mechanical failure. |
| ENGINE CRANKS BUT WILL NOT START—NO SMOKE FROM EXHAUST | 1. No Fuel in supply tank.<br>2. Air intake or exhaust systems plugged.<br>3. Fuel filter plugged.<br>4. Excessive fuel inlet restriction.<br>5. Injection pump is not getting fuel or fuel is aerated.<br>6. Inoperative fuel transfer (lift) pump.<br>7. One or more injectors worn or not working properly.<br>8. Worn or inoperative injection pump.<br>9. Internal pump timing incorrect.<br>10. Camshaft out of time. | 1. Fill fuel supply.<br>2. Remove obstruction.<br>3. Drain fuel canister and replace filter.<br>4. Check fuel inlet restriction. Correct cause.<br>5. Check fuel flow/bleed fuel system.<br>6. Check fuel lines for restrictions and fuel pressure.<br>7. Check/replaced bad or improperly operating injectors.<br>8. Visually check delivery with externally connected injector to one of the pump outlets. Repair or replace the pump if fuel is not being delivered.<br>9. Time the injection pump. Refer to group 14, fuel system.<br>10. Check/correct gear train timing alignment. |
| ENGINE HARD TO START OR WILL NOT START—SMOKE FROM EXHAUST | 1. Incorrect starting procedure.<br>2. Cranking speed too slow.<br>3. Intake heater system not working.<br>4. Insufficient intake air. | 1. the fuel shut off solenoid control must be in the run position.<br>2. Check the battery, starter motor and wiring for loose connections.<br>3. Verify system is working. Repair/replace inoperative parts.<br>4. Inspect or replace filter and check for obstructions to the air supply tube. |
